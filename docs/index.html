üìä Project Overview

Air pollution is a major public health concern in urban environments. This project examines how fine particulate matter (PM2.5) relates to asthma emergency department visits in New York City, combining time-series analysis, spatial modeling, and statistical inference to understand environmental health patterns.

Key Research Questions

  1. Temporal Trends: How have PM2.5 levels and asthma rates changed from 2009 to 2023?
  2. Geographic Patterns: How do these outcomes vary across NYC‚Äôs five boroughs?
  3. Statistical Association: What is the relationship between PM2.5 and asthma, controlling for confounders?
  4. Environmental Justice: Which communities experience disproportionate health burdens?

Project Overview

Air pollution is a major public health concern in urban environments, and New York City consistently reports high rates of respiratory illnesses, particularly asthma. Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) is known to trigger asthma symptoms and increase emergency department (ED) visits.

This project explores how PM2.5 levels vary across time and NYC boroughs, and whether increases in pollution are associated with higher asthma ED visit rates.

Research Questions
  1. Temporal Trends: How have PM2.5 levels and asthma ED visit rates changed over time in NYC (2009‚Äì2023)?
  2. Geographic Disparities: Do boroughs differ in both PM2.5 concentration and asthma ED visit rates?
  3. Association Analysis: Is higher PM2.5 associated with higher asthma ED visit rates after adjusting for seasonality and borough?
  4. Effect Modification: Does season modify the association between PM2.5 and asthma ED visits?
